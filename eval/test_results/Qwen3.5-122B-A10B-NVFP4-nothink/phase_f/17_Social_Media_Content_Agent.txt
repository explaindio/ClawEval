### 4. Reddit (r/programming)
**Focus:** Authenticity, technical details, and avoiding "hype."

**Title:** We built an AI coding assistant that actually respects local context (and doesn't leak your code). Launching today.

**Body:**
Hey everyone,

I've been lurking here for years and have seen a lot of "AI coding" tools come and go. Most of them feel like glorified autocomplete that hallucinates libraries or, worse, sends your proprietary code to a public training set.

My team and I spent the last 18 months building **CodePilot**. We took a different approach:
1. **Local-First Architecture:** You can run the inference engine locally (via Docker) so your code never leaves your machine unless you explicitly opt-in to cloud features.
2. **Repo-Awareness:** Instead of just looking at the open file, it indexes your whole project structure to understand dependencies and architecture patterns.
3. **No "Black Box":** Every suggestion comes with a diff view and an explanation of *why* it made that change, so you can review it like a PR.

We aren't trying to replace devs; we just want to stop us from wasting 2 hours on regex or boilerplate. We're open-sourcing the core inference adapter today and launching the beta.
