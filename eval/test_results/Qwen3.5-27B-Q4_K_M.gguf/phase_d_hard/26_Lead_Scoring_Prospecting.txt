### **Detailed Reasoning**

**1. Lead 4 (Score: 95) – The Best Prospect**
*   **Why:** This lead checks every box in the BANT framework (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline). They are in Procurement (Authority), mentioned Q2 budget (Budget/Timeline), and asked about SOC 2/Volume discounts (Need).
*   **Action:** Immediate sales outreach. They are ready to close.

**2. Lead 3 (Score: 85) – The Product-Led Winner**
*   **Why:** This is a high-quality Product Qualified Lead (PQL). They aren't just reading about the product; they built a POC and invited teammates. Asking about SSO integration signals they are moving from "testing" to "enterprise deployment."
*   **Action:** Engage the developer to facilitate the handoff to procurement/security. High conversion probability.

**3. Lead 5 (Score: 35) – The Churned Trialist**
*   **Why:** A Founder is a decision-maker, which is good. However, 6 weeks of silence after a trial indicates they either didn't find value or lost interest. They are salvageable with a re-engagement campaign, but the probability of conversion is low.
*   **Action:** Send a "We miss you" email with a case study, but do not prioritize over Leads 3 or 4.

**4. Lead 2 (Score: 20) – The Stale Lead**
*   **Why:** A pricing page visit is a strong signal, but it happened 3 months ago. Without any follow-up activity, the intent has likely evaporated.
*   **Action:** Add to a nurture stream. Do not waste sales time calling them yet.

**5. Lead 1 (Score: 15) – The Tire-Kicker**
*   **Why:** This lead is the trap. Their engagement metrics (12 whitepapers, 18 months) look impressive on a dashboard, but they are **negative signals**. They are hoarding information without acting. They have explicitly stated they have no timeline.
*   **Action:** **Stop calling.** Move them to a long-term nurture list. If you chase them, you will waste resources on a lead that is using your content for free without intending to pay.
