## PROMPT:
Analyze this clinical trial data. WARNING: The obvious conclusion from the aggregate data is WRONG. You must analyze the subgroups to find the truth. This is a real statistical phenomenon.

Overall Results:
- Treatment A: 100 patients, 40 recovered (40%)
- Treatment B: 100 patients, 50 recovered (50%)

Subgroup breakdown:
- Mild cases with Treatment A: 10 patients, 8 recovered (80%)
- Mild cases with Treatment B: 60 patients, 45 recovered (75%)
- Severe cases with Treatment A: 90 patients, 32 recovered (35.6%)
- Severe cases with Treatment B: 40 patients, 5 recovered (12.5%)

Questions:
1. Based on aggregate data alone, which treatment appears better?
2. When you look at subgroups, which treatment is actually better for mild cases?
3. Which treatment is actually better for severe cases?
4. Explain the paradox — why does the aggregate data mislead?
5. What is the name of this statistical phenomenon?
6. What is your final recommendation and why?

## RESPONSE:
Here is the analysis of the clinical trial data based on your requirements.

**1. Based on aggregate data alone, which treatment appears better?**
Based solely on the overall results, **Treatment B appears better**. It shows a 50% recovery rate compared to Treatment A's 40% recovery rate.

**2. When you look at subgroups, which treatment is actually better for mild cases?**
For mild cases, **Treatment A is better**. It achieved an 80% recovery rate, compared to Treatment B's 75% recovery rate.

**3. Which treatment is actually better for severe cases?**
For severe cases, **Treatment A is better**. It achieved a 35.6% recovery rate, compared to Treatment B's 12.5% recovery rate.

**4. Explain the paradox — why does the aggregate data mislead?**
The aggregate data is misleading due to a **confounding variable**: the severity of the illness.
*   **Treatment A** was administered predominantly to severe cases (90 out of 100 patients). Since severe cases are harder to treat, the overall recovery rate for Treatment A is dragged down, even though it performs well within that specific group.
*   **Treatment B** was administered predominantly to mild cases (60 out of 100 patients). Since mild cases are easier to treat, the overall recovery rate for Treatment B is artificially inflated.

When you combine the groups without accounting for severity, Treatment B looks superior simply because it was tested on a healthier population, not because the drug is more effective.

**5. What is the name of this statistical phenomenon?**
This is known as **Simpson's Paradox**. It occurs when a trend appears in different groups of data but disappears or reverses when these groups are combined.

**6. What is your final recommendation and why?**
**Recommendation: Use Treatment A.**

**Reasoning:**
Treatment A is statistically superior in **both** subgroups (Mild and Severe). The lower aggregate score for Treatment A is an artifact of the patient distribution (it was tested on sicker patients), not a reflection of the drug's efficacy. To make a valid clinical decision, one must control for the confounding variable (severity). Since Treatment A yields better outcomes regardless of the patient's condition, it is the medically superior choice.
