### Executive Summary: The "Hidden" Trends

Before answering the specific questions, here are the critical trends that are likely being ignored by stakeholders:

1.  **Exponential "Carry-Over Debt":** The number of unfinished stories is doubling every sprint (1 → 3 → 6). This is not linear; it is exponential. This indicates the team is losing the ability to close loops, leading to massive context switching and cognitive load.
2.  **The "Scope Creep vs. Velocity" Inverse Correlation:** As mid-sprint additions increased (0 → 3 → 5 stories), the team's actual velocity *dropped* (30 → 32 → 28). This proves that adding work mid-sprint is actively destroying productivity, not adding value.
3.  **Planning Fallacy Acceleration:** The team is planning for more stories every sprint (8 → 10 → 12) despite completing fewer stories (7 → 7 → 6). The "Planned vs. Completed" gap is widening from 4 pts (S1) to 20 pts (S3).
4.  **Consistent Estimation, Failing Execution:** The average story size is consistently **4 points** across all sprints (34/8 ≈ 4.25, 40/10 = 4, 48/12 = 4). The drop in velocity is **not** due to bad estimation; it is due to interruptions and focus loss.

### 1. Calculate the velocity trend across 3 sprints (exact numbers)

Velocity is defined as the sum of Story Points **Completed** and accepted during the sprint.

*   **Sprint 1 Velocity:** 30 pts
*   **Sprint 2 Velocity:** 32 pts
*   **Sprint 3 Velocity:** 28 pts
*   **Average Velocity:** (30 + 32 + 28) / 3 = **30 pts/sprint**
*   **Velocity Trend:** **Declining.** While Sprint 2 showed a slight bump (+2), Sprint 3 dropped significantly (-4). The trend line is moving downward as interruptions increase.

### 2. What is the realistic capacity for Sprint 4?

Realistic capacity is based on historical average velocity, adjusted for current team health and backlog debt.

*   **Base Capacity:** 30 pts (3-sprint average).
*   **Adjustment for Carry-Over:** Sprint 3 ended with 6 stories carried over. At 4 pts/story (based on historical average), this is **24 pts** of existing debt that *must* be finished before new value can be delivered.
*   **Net Capacity for New Work:** 30 pts (Total Capacity) - 24 pts (Carry-Over Debt) = **6 pts**.

**Realistic Capacity:**
*   **Total Work Capacity:** 30 pts.
*   **Capacity for *New* Sprint 4 Stories:** **6 pts (approx. 1-2 stories).**

### 4. Is the Sprint 4 commitment achievable? Show math.

**No, it is mathematically impossible.**

*   **Commitment:** 60 pts (15 stories).
*   **Historical Average Velocity:** 30 pts.
*   **Required Performance:** 60 / 30 = **200% of historical capacity.**

If the team attempts to deliver the 60 pts of *new* work while ignoring the 6 stories carried over from Sprint 3 (24 pts):

*   **Team Capacity:** 30 pts.
*   **Completion Rate:** 30 / 84 = **35.7%**
