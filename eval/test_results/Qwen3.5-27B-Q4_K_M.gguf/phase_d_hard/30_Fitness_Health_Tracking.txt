## RESPONSE:
### Analysis of Fitness Log

#### 1. Warning Signs of Overtraining
The user is exhibiting classic symptoms of **Non-Functional Overreaching** bordering on **Overtraining Syndrome (OTS)**. The specific warning signs include:

*   **Elevated Resting Heart Rate (RHR):** RHR climbed steadily from 58 bpm to 67 bpm in Week 1 (+9 bpm). This indicates autonomic nervous system stress (sympathetic dominance) and insufficient recovery.
*   **Sleep Deprivation:** Averaging 5.5 hours in Week 1 and dropping to 5 hours in Week 2 is critically low for this training volume. Recovery happens during sleep, not during workouts.
*   **Performance Decline:** Week 2 shows a clear drop in output: slower run times (52min vs 48min), inability to complete weight sets ("felt weak"), and skipping workouts ("too tired").
*   **Injury Onset:** The appearance of knee pain in Week 2 is a direct result of cumulative fatigue and compromised biomechanics.
*   **Mental Disconnect:** The desire to "push harder next week to make up for the bad week" despite physical failure is a psychological hallmark of overtraining.

#### 2. The Data Inconsistency
**The Inconsistency:** **Reported Caloric Intake (3,500/day) vs. Weight Loss (4 lbs in 2 weeks).**

*   **Why it is inconsistent:** 3,500 calories is a high intake, typically sufficient for maintenance or a surplus for an active individual. Losing 4 lbs of body mass in 14 days requires a deficit of approximately 14,000 calories (1,000 calories/day).
*   **The Reality:** While mathematically possible if the user's TDEE is extremely high, in the context of overtraining, this discrepancy usually indicates **under-reporting of food intake**. The user likely believes they are eating 3,500 calories but is actually in a significant deficit. Alternatively, the weight loss is due to **muscle catabolism** (breaking down muscle for energy) caused by high cortisol levels from stress and lack of sleep, rather than fat loss. Either way, the claim of "eating enough" contradicts the physiological outcome of rapid weight loss and weakness.

#### 3. Advice
**Do not train more.** The user is currently in a catabolic state and risking long-term injury.

1.  **Immediate Deload:** Stop all structured training for 7–10 days. No running, no weights, no HIIT. Light walking only if it does not cause pain.
2.  **Prioritize Sleep:** Increase sleep to 7–9 hours per night. This is non-negotiable for lowering RHR and repairing tissue.
3.  **Verify Nutrition:** The user is likely under-fueling. They should track food intake strictly for 3 days to verify the 3,500 calorie claim. Given the weight loss and fatigue, they likely need to increase intake, not decrease it.
4.  **Medical Assessment:** The knee pain requires evaluation by a physiotherapist or doctor to rule out stress fractures or tendonitis before returning to running.
5.  **Mindset Shift:** "Making up" for a bad week by training harder is the exact behavior that caused the crash. Recovery *is* the work right now.
