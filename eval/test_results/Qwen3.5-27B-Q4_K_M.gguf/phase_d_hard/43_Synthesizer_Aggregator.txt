## PROMPT:
Three agents analyzed whether to open a second office location. Their analyses CONTRADICT each other on key points. You must synthesize — not just summarize — and make a recommendation.

Agent A (Financial): "Opening in Austin will cost $2M upfront. Break-even in 18 months. ROI of 35% by year 3. However, our cash reserves are only $3M and we need $1M buffer. Recommendation: GO."

Agent B (HR/Talent): "Austin has a strong tech talent pool but salaries are 20% higher than expected. Retention at remote offices historically 30% lower in first 2 years. We'd need 15 people, not the 10 originally planned, due to the retention gap. Recommendation: DELAY 6 months."

Agent C (Strategy): "3 competitors opened Austin offices last year. Market is becoming saturated. However, NOT having a presence could mean losing 2 key enterprise clients who prefer local teams. Those clients represent 25% of revenue. Recommendation: GO but with a smaller team."

Note: Agent A's financial model assumes 10 people (not 15), Agent B's retention data contradicts Agent A's break-even timeline, and Agent C's competitor info wasn't available to Agent A.

Synthesize into ONE recommendation. Show how you resolved the contradictions.

## RESPONSE:
**Recommendation: CONDITIONAL GO (Phased Launch)**

**Decision:** Proceed with opening the Austin office immediately, but **do not** use Agent A's financial model or Agent B's headcount plan. Instead, execute a lean, phased entry to secure the enterprise clients (Agent C) while protecting cash reserves (Agent A) and mitigating retention risks (Agent B).

### Synthesis & Contradiction Resolution

**1. Resolving the Financial vs. Talent Contradiction (Agent A vs. Agent B)**
*   **The Conflict:** Agent A's $2M budget assumes 10 employees. Agent B states we need 15 employees to account for a 30% retention gap and 20% higher salaries. If we follow Agent B's headcount, the $2M budget is insufficient, violating Agent A's cash buffer requirement ($1M).
*   **The Resolution:** We cannot afford 15 people upfront. We must adopt Agent C's suggestion of a "smaller team" (approx. 10-12) but budget for **recruitment costs** rather than permanent headcount.
    *   *Action:* Hire 10 core staff (matching Agent A's budget) but allocate 15% of the $2M budget to a "turnover contingency fund" for aggressive recruitment and signing bonuses. This acknowledges Agent B's retention data without burning cash on 5 permanent salaries immediately.

**2. Resolving the Timing vs. Strategy Contradiction (Agent B vs. Agent C)**
*   **The Conflict:** Agent B recommends delaying 6 months to prepare for talent challenges. Agent C warns that delaying risks losing 25% of total revenue (2 key clients).
*   **The Resolution:** The revenue risk (25%) is existential; the talent risk is operational. We cannot delay.
    *   *Action:* Launch immediately to secure the enterprise clients (Agent C). However, to address Agent B's concern, we will implement a "remote-first" hybrid model for the first 6 months. This allows us to test the Austin market and hire talent remotely before committing to full physical office overhead, effectively achieving Agent B's "delay" benefit without losing the clients.

**3. Resolving the Market Saturation vs. Investment Contradiction (Agent A vs. Agent C)**
*   **The Conflict:** Agent A assumes standard ROI (35% by Year 3). Agent C notes the market is saturated with 3 new competitors, which will likely compress margins and extend the break-even timeline.
*   **The Resolution:** Agent A's 18-month break-even is overly optimistic given Agent C's market data.
    *   *Action:* Revise the financial model to a **24-month break-even** and a **25% ROI by Year 3**. This adjusts Agent A's expectations to reflect the competitive reality Agent C identified, ensuring we do not over-leverage our cash reserves based on flawed projections.

### Final Action Plan

1.  **Budget Adjustment:** Cap upfront spend at $1.8M (leaving $1.2M buffer) to account for the 20% salary increase identified by Agent B.
2.  **Headcount Strategy:** Hire 10 core staff immediately (Agent A's baseline) but authorize a recruitment budget to replace attrition (Agent B's risk) rather than over-hiring.
3.  **Client Assurance:** Assign a senior account manager to the two key enterprise clients immediately to guarantee local presence (Agent C's priority).
4.  **Timeline:** Launch office operations in 30 days. Re-evaluate headcount expansion to 15 people only after Month 6, contingent on client retention and cash flow.

**Verdict:** The risk of losing 25% of revenue outweighs the risk of talent attrition. We must **GO**, but we must correct Agent A's financial assumptions and Agent B's staffing assumptions to ensure we do not run out of cash before hitting break-even.
