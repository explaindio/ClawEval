## RESPONSE:
### 5-Year Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Analysis

To determine the best value, we must calculate the cost to maintain a functional laptop for a full **5-year period**. This includes the initial purchase, necessary accessories, warranty fees, battery replacements, and the cost of replacing the laptop itself if its lifespan is shorter than 5 years.

**Assumptions:**
*   **Accessories:** Docks and adapters are assumed to be reusable across laptop generations (one-time cost). External SSDs are assumed to be reusable storage (one-time cost).
*   **Battery:** Batteries degrade over time. We budget for **1 replacement** for any laptop expected to last more than 3 years.
*   **Laptop Replacement:** If a laptop's lifespan is less than 5 years, a new unit must be purchased to cover the remaining time.
*   **Warranty:** For Product B, the extended warranty is included to guarantee the 5-year lifespan against unexpected failures.

### 1. Product A — Laptop Alpha
*   **Sticker Price:** $899
*   **Lifespan:** 3 Years (Requires 2 units for 5 years)
*   **Accessories:** $150 Dock + $80 Adapter = $230 (One-time)
*   **Battery:** Requires 1 replacement ($200) to reach end of lifespan.
*   **Warranty:** None.

**Math:**
*   Laptop Unit 1 (Years 1-3): $899
*   Laptop Unit 2 (Years 4-5): $899
*   Accessories: $230
*   Battery Replacement: $200
*   **Total TCO:** $899 + $899 + $230 + $200 = **$2,228**

### Recommendation

**Best Value: Product B (Laptop Beta)**

Despite having the highest sticker price ($1,299), **Product B** is the most cost-effective option over a 5-year period.

**Why Product B Wins:**
1.  **No Replacement Costs:** It is the only laptop with a 5-year lifespan, eliminating the need to buy a second or third unit.
2.  **No Accessories:** It includes all necessary ports, saving $80–$430 in peripheral costs compared to the other models.
3.  **Lower Battery Cost:** The replacement battery is significantly cheaper ($80) compared to Alpha ($200) and Delta ($150).

**Why Product C is the Trap:**
Product C has the lowest sticker price ($749), but it is the **most expensive** option in the long run ($2,677). Its short 2-year lifespan forces you to buy three laptops in five years, and the required external SSD adds recurring storage costs. This confirms the warning: the cheapest option is not the best value.
