### 🔴 Red Flag Analysis

This job posting exhibits **8 critical red flags**, ranging from wage theft to impossible work expectations.

#### 1. The "Unicorn" Tech Stack (Impossible Expectation)
*   **The Flag:** The requirements list 22 distinct technologies across frontend, backend, infrastructure, and DevOps (e.g., React, Vue, Angular, Svelte, Rust, Go, Azure, GCP, AWS).
*   **Why it's a problem:** No single human can be an expert in all of these. This indicates the company is looking for a "unicorn" to do the work of 3-4 different roles (Frontend, Backend, DevOps, Data Engineer) for one salary. It sets the candidate up for immediate failure and burnout.

#### 2. Contradictory Experience Level
*   **The Flag:** The listing claims "Entry level" but requires "5+ years experience."
*   **Why it's a problem:** This is a lie designed to attract senior talent at junior pay rates. "Entry level" implies training and mentorship; 5+ years implies autonomy and leadership. They want senior output without senior compensation.

#### 3. Wage Theft via Unpaid Labor
*   **The Flag:** A "coding challenge (estimated 40 hours)."
*   **Why it's a problem:** A 40-hour coding challenge is effectively a full week of unpaid work. This is a common tactic to extract free code or solutions for their actual product. Legitimate companies typically cap take-home tests at 2-4 hours.

#### 4. "Family" Culture & Burnout Hours
*   **The Flag:** "Mandatory 60hr weeks... approximately 48 weeks/year" + "We're like a family!"
*   **Why it's a problem:**
    *   **Math:** 60 hours/week for 48 weeks/year = 2,880 hours/year. A standard full-time year is ~2,080 hours. This is a **38% increase** over standard work hours.
    *   **Manipulation:** Using "family" to justify 60-hour weeks is emotional manipulation. Families do not demand 48 weeks of overtime annually. This is a guaranteed path to burnout and health issues.

#### 5. Meaningless Equity & Toxic Vesting
*   **The Flag:** "0.001% equity vesting over 6 years with 2-year cliff."
*   **Why it's a problem:**
    *   **Value:** 0.001% is negligible. Even if the company exits for $100M (unlikely for a stealth startup), this candidate receives $1,000.
    *   **Terms:** A 6-year vesting schedule is non-standard (standard is 4 years). A 2-year cliff means if they leave or get fired before year 2, they get **zero** equity. This is a "golden handcuff" designed to trap you.

#### 6. The "Remote" Time Zone Trap
*   **The Flag:** "Remote* (*must be available 6am-midnight PT for 'flexibility')"
*   **Why it's a problem:** This is not remote work; it is on-call work. If you are in a different time zone, you are expected to work 18-hour days to cover that window. It eliminates work-life balance and suggests they want you available whenever the US West Coast team wakes up or stays up late.

#### 7. "Unlimited PTO" Reality
*   **The Flag:** "Unlimited PTO (average team usage: 5 days/year)."
*   **Why it's a problem:** Unlimited PTO is often code for "No PTO." The parenthetical admission that the team only takes 5 days a year proves that taking time off is culturally discouraged or penalized.

#### 8. Lack of Company Transparency
*   **The Flag:** "Stealth Startup (unnamed)"
*   **Why it's a problem:** Legitimate companies are proud of their brand. An unnamed company implies they are hiding something (legal issues, failed pivots, or a toxic reputation). It also makes it impossible to research the founders or the product before applying, increasing the risk of fraud.
