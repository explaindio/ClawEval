## PROMPT:
Handle this support conversation. The customer has a LEGITIMATE complaint but is making 2 unreasonable demands. You must identify which demands are reasonable vs unreasonable WITHOUT making the customer feel dismissed.

Customer: "I've been a premium subscriber for 4 years paying $99/month. Three times this month, I lost work because your auto-save failed. I want:
1. A full refund for this month ($99)
2. Free premium for the next 12 months as compensation
3. A personal guarantee from your CEO that this won't happen again
4. My files recovered from before the auto-save failures
5. The developer who wrote the auto-save code to be fired

I've already posted on Twitter about this and I'm considering legal action."

Respond professionally. You CAN offer: refund for current month, 1-3 months credit, priority support, escalation to engineering. You CANNOT promise: firing employees, CEO personal guarantees, or more than 3 months free. File recovery may or may not be possible.

## RESPONSE:
**Subject:** Urgent: Resolution regarding your Auto-Save Incident & Account Status

Dear [Customer Name],

Thank you for reaching out. First and foremost, I want to sincerely apologize for the frustration and work loss you experienced this month. Losing access to your data due to an auto-save failure is unacceptable, especially for a valued customer who has trusted us for four years. I understand the gravity of this situation and the impact it has had on your workflow.

I have personally reviewed your account and the incident logs. I want to address each of your points directly and outline the immediate steps we are taking to resolve this.

**1. Refund for Current Month**
I have processed a full refund for this month's subscription ($99) immediately. You should see this reflected in your original payment method within 3-5 business days.

**2. Compensation for Service Interruption**
To make up for the disruption and the time lost, I am authorized to extend your Premium subscription for the next **three months** at no cost. While I cannot approve a twelve-month extension due to system limitations, I have secured the maximum discretionary credit available to ensure you have ample time to verify system stability moving forward.

**3. File Recovery**
Our engineering team is currently running a forensic scan on your account to locate any pre-failure versions of your files. I have flagged this as a high-priority ticket. I will provide you with a status update within 24 hours regarding whether recovery is possible.

**4. System Assurance & Leadership Involvement**
Regarding your request for a personal guarantee: While I cannot speak on behalf of the CEO or provide personal guarantees, I am escalating this incident directly to our **VP of Engineering**. They will be reviewing the auto-save architecture personally to ensure this does not recur. You will receive a direct update from them once the review is complete.

**5. Personnel Actions**
We take system reliability very seriously. Our focus is on identifying and fixing the root cause of the technical failure rather than individual personnel decisions. Rest assured, the engineering team is fully committed to implementing the necessary safeguards to prevent this from happening again.

**Next Steps**
*   **Priority Support:** I have added your account to our Priority Support queue. Any future tickets will be handled by a senior specialist.
*   **Twitter/Legal:** We prefer to resolve these matters directly with our customers to ensure you get the fastest possible solution. I am confident that the steps above demonstrate our commitment to making this right.

I know this does not undo the lost work, but I hope this demonstrates our dedication to regaining your trust. I will be personally monitoring this ticket until it is fully resolved.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
Senior Customer Success Manager
[Company Name]
