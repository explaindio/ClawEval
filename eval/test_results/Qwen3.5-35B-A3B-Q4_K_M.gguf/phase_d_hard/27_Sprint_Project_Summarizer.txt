### 2. What is the realistic capacity for Sprint 4?

**Realistic Capacity: 30 Points (New Work)**

**The Math:**
*   **Historical Velocity:** 30 pts (Average of last 3 sprints).
*   **Carry-Over Debt:** Sprint 3 ended with **6 stories carried over**.
    *   Based on the average story size (48 pts / 12 stories = 4 pts/story), these 6 stories represent **24 pts of debt**.
*   **Net Capacity:** If the team must clear the 24 pts of debt before starting new work, their capacity for *new* value is:
    *   30 pts (Velocity) - 24 pts (Debt) = **6 pts of new work.**

**Conclusion:** Even if they ignore the debt, the realistic capacity for *new* value is **30 pts**. If they must clear the backlog, it is **6 pts**.

### 3. What percentage of scope creep has occurred each sprint?

Scope creep is calculated as **(Mid-Sprint Added Points / Original Planned Points) × 100**.
*Assumption: Average story size is consistent at 4 pts (derived from Sprint 1 & 3).*

*   **Sprint 1:** 0 stories added / 34 pts = **0%**
*   **Sprint 2:** 3 stories added (12 pts) / 40 pts = **30%**
*   **Sprint 3:** 5 stories added (20 pts) / 48 pts = **41.6%**

**The Hidden Trend:** Scope creep is not just happening; it is **accelerating**. The Product team is adding 30% to 40% more work mid-sprint, yet the team is finishing *less* of the original plan. This indicates that mid-sprint additions are directly cannibalizing the team's ability to finish committed work.

### 4. Is the Sprint 4 commitment achievable? Show math.

**Verdict: NO. It is mathematically impossible.**

**The Commitment:** 15 stories (60 pts).
**The Reality:**
1.  **Velocity Gap:** The team's average velocity is 30 pts. The commitment is **60 pts**. This requires **200% of their historical capacity**.
2.  **Carry-Over Tax:** There are 6 stories from Sprint 3 (approx. 24 pts) that must be completed before new work begins.
3.  **Total Work Required:** 60 pts (New Commitment) + 24 pts (Carry-Over) = **84 pts**.
4.  **Required Velocity:** To hit this in one sprint, the team needs to deliver **84 pts**.
5.  **Current Velocity:** **30 pts**.

**Math:**
$$ \text{Feasibility} = \frac{\text{Current Velocity}}{\text{Required Work}} = \frac{30}{84} = \mathbf{35.7\%} $$

**Conclusion:** The team is only capable of delivering **35%** of the requested work. Even if they ignore the carry-over, they are only capable of delivering 50% of the new commitment.

### 5. What should you recommend to the product owner?

**Recommendation 1: Stop the Mid-Sprint Injections Immediately**
The data proves a direct correlation between mid-sprint additions and failed commitments.
*   **Action:** Enforce a "Sprint Freeze." No stories added after Sprint Planning Day 1. If a story is urgent, it must replace a story of equal size from the current plan.

**Recommendation 2: Address the Carry-Over Exponential**
The carry-over is not linear (1, 3, 6); it is accelerating.
*   **Action:** Dedicate Sprint 4 entirely to clearing the 6 carried-over stories (approx. 24 pts). Do not plan new work until the backlog is cleared.

**Recommendation 3: Renegotiate the "Non-Negotiable" Deadline**
A deadline cannot be met if the scope is inflated by 40% mid-sprint and the velocity is half the requirement.
*   **Action:** Present the math (30 pts vs 60 pts commitment). Offer a trade: "We can meet the deadline if we cut the scope to 30 pts, OR we can deliver the 60 pts if we extend the timeline by 2 sprints."

**Recommendation 4: Protect the Team**
The team is currently operating in a "crisis mode" that is unsustainable.
*   **Action:** Cancel Sprint 4 planning until the Product Owner agrees to the Scope Freeze. Continuing to plan for 60 pts will result in a 0% completion rate and burnout.
