### Explanation of Hidden Urgency

Two emails contain **hidden urgency clues** buried within their text that elevate them from routine updates to critical priorities:

1.  **Email 6 (P2):** The subject line ("Meeting notes") and opening ("Attached are the notes") suggest a routine, read-only communication. However, the **hidden urgency** is buried in the second sentence: *"the CFO mentioned they're freezing all new hires starting Monday."* This is a critical strategic decision impacting expansion plans with an immediate deadline (Monday), requiring immediate review of hiring pipelines.
2.  **Email 3 (P3):** The subject line ("FYI") and opening ("Just letting you know Acme renewed") suggest a low-priority administrative update. However, the **hidden urgency** is in the PS: *"their contract has a 30-day termination clause that activates next week."* This indicates a significant business risk where a key vendor could leave or renegotiate terms very soon, requiring immediate retention or contingency action.

**Ranking Logic:**
*   **P1 (Email 2):** Immediate deadline ("tomorrow") with high-stakes consequences (Board presentation with wrong data).
*   **P2 (Email 6):** Strategic impact with a near-term deadline ("Monday").
*   **P3 (Email 3):** Strategic risk with a short-term deadline ("next week").
*   **P4 (Email 4):** Operational necessity marked "URGENT" in the subject, but lower business impact than the strategic risks above.
*   **Emails 1, 5, 7, 8:** Lower priority (Social, Confirmation, Automated, Future Notice).
