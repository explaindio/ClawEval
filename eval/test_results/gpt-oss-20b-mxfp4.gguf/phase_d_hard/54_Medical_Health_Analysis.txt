## RESPONSE:
**1. Dangerous interaction to discuss with your doctor**

- *What are the risks of adding daily aspirin to my current warfarin therapy?*  
  Aspirin and warfarin both inhibit platelet function and can synergistically increase the risk of bleeding.  Because warfarin’s effect is monitored by the INR, adding aspirin can cause the INR to rise above the therapeutic range (2.0‑3.0) and heighten bleeding risk.  Ask your provider how you should monitor the INR more closely and whether any dose adjustment of warfarin or aspirin is needed.

---

**2. Inconsistency in the lab results**

- *Why am I taking Metformin when my fasting glucose is 95 mg/dL and my A1C is 5.4%?*  
  Metformin is typically prescribed for type 2 diabetes, yet the glucose and A1C values are within normal limits.  Discuss whether Metformin is still necessary, whether the medication was prescribed for another indication, or whether a medication change is warranted.

**3. Blood‑pressure control and implications for Lisinopril**

- *My blood pressure is 145/92 mmHg—does this mean my lisinopril dose is insufficient?*  
  The target systolic pressure for most adults is <130 mmHg, and the diastolic target is <80 mmHg.  A reading of 145/92 mmHg indicates that blood‑pressure control is not optimal.  Ask whether the lisinopril dose should be increased, whether an additional antihypertensive should be added, or whether lifestyle modifications might help.
