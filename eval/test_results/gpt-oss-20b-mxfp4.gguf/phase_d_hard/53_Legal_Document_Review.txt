### Flagged Issue: Clause 6 Hidden Escalation

**Why it matters**  
Clause 6 reads:  
> “Upon each renewal, pricing increases by the greater of: (a) 15 %, or (b) the applicable rate increase as determined solely by Provider.”

The second option (“determined solely by Provider”) is a *hidden* escalation mechanism that can lead to price hikes far beyond 15 % without any customer input.  Because the clause is buried under the renewal language, a customer may overlook it until the price jump occurs.

**What to do**  
- **Cap the increase** (e.g., 10 % or inflation‑based).  
- **Require mutual agreement** or a transparent formula.  
- **Provide a longer notice period** (at least 60 days).  
- **Add a clause that allows the customer to negotiate or terminate** if the increase is unreasonable.
