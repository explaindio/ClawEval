*Interpretation*

**Implication:** Aggressive revenue recognition or “round‑tripping” of sales to a related party (see Note 14) is likely.  A DSO that high is unsustainable and often signals that revenue is being booked before the cash is actually received.

**4. Significance of Note 14**

> “$22 M of Y3 revenue from transactions with CEO’s brother’s company”

- **22 M / 130 M = 16.9 %** of total revenue.  
- A *related‑party transaction* that accounts for a sizeable chunk of sales.  
- Raises **conflict‑of‑interest** concerns and the possibility that the company is inflating sales to a friendly entity.  
- The large receivable (45 M) includes this 22 M, further inflating DSO.

**Bottom line:** The note signals a potential earnings‑manipulation channel and a governance risk.
