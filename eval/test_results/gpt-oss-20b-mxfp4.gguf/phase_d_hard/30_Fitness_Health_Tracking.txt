## What to do next – “Not train more”

| Goal | Practical steps |
|------|-----------------|
| **Prioritize recovery** | • **Add 1–2 full rest days** per week (no running, no heavy weights). <br>• Replace “active recovery” with light walking or gentle yoga only. |
| **Improve sleep hygiene** | • Keep a consistent bedtime (same time every night). <br>• Limit caffeine after 2 pm. <br>• Create a dark, cool sleep environment. <br>• Consider a short pre‑sleep wind‑down routine (reading, breathing). |
| **Re‑evaluate nutrition** | • Use a food‑tracking app (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer) to log *actual* calories, macronutrients, and micronutrients. <br>• Aim for a slight deficit (≈200–300 kcal) if you want to keep losing weight, but ensure 1.2–1.6 g protein/kg body weight. <br>• If you’re truly eating 3,500 kcal, you’re likely in a surplus – this will need adjustment. |
| **Monitor training load** | • Use RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) or heart‑rate zones to keep sessions in the 60–75 % HRmax range for most runs. <br>• Track weekly mileage and keep increases <10 % per week. |
| **Address knee pain** | • Stop high‑impact running until pain resolves. <br>• Add low‑impact cardio (bike, swim) for 20–30 min. <br>• Strengthen the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and hip abductors with body‑weight or resistance exercises. <br>• Consider a sports‑medicine consult if pain persists. |
| **Re‑introduce weights gradually** | • Start with 50–60 % of usual load for 2–3 sets, 8–10 reps. <br>• Focus on form, not volume. |
| **Track progress** | • Keep a simple log: distance, pace, RHR, sleep hours, perceived fatigue (1–10). <br>• Review weekly to spot trends early. |
| **Mental recovery** | • Include 5–10 min of mindfulness or breathing each day. <br>• Keep training fun—mix in a new route or a different activity. |

### Bottom line

Your body is telling you that the current volume and intensity are too high for the recovery you’re getting. The “push harder next week” mindset will likely backfire, leading to more injury or a plateau. Instead:

1. **Scale back** – cut mileage, drop the heavy weight day, and add a real rest day.  
2. **Re‑balance** – get enough sleep, track calories accurately, and ensure adequate protein.  
3. **Heal** – treat the knee pain with rest, mobility work, and strength training.  

Once you’re back on a sustainable rhythm, you can gradually re‑introduce volume and intensity—ideally with a periodized plan that builds in recovery weeks. This approach will help you keep losing weight safely, avoid injury, and actually make the “push harder” goal achievable in the long run.
