**Professional Product‑Shot Overview**

A high‑end studio photograph of the “premium wireless gaming mouse” should feel sleek, technical, and slightly futuristic. The image is **crisp, high‑resolution (≈ 50 MP), with a shallow depth‑of‑field that keeps the mouse tack‑sharp while a very gentle blur falls off on the cable and background**. The lighting is **balanced‑studio** – a strong key light, soft fill, a subtle rim‑light for separation, and a low‑intensity cyan‑LED that catches the side‑strip glow. The mouse sits on a **minimal, dark‑gray cyclorama** that fades into a near‑black background, ensuring the RGB accent pops without distracting reflections.

Below is a step‑by‑step breakdown you can hand to a photographer, a CGI artist, or an AI image‑generation model.

---

## 1. Exact Visual Layout

| Element | Position | Size / Proportion | Notes |

On a 4 × 5‑inch sensor (or 4K display) the mouse occupies ~70 % of the vertical frame and ~55 % of the horizontal. | The mouse is placed so the **front (USB‑C) edge** faces the left side of the frame, giving a natural “pointing” direction. |
| **Cable (detachable braided paracord)** | Loosely coiled on the right side of the mouse, with a gentle “U” shape that leads the eye toward the mouse’s front. | The coil is about 30 % of the mouse’s length; the cable’s thickness is ~2 mm, with a visible braided texture. | The cable is **detached** but shown neatly arranged to suggest “ready‑to‑use”. The braiding is a tight weave of matte black fibers with a subtle sheen under the key light. |

| **Shadow** | Soft, elongated shadow under the mouse, extending toward the lower‑right corner. | ~20 % of the mouse length; diffuse edges with a slight falloff. | Created by a small, translucent reflector placed beneath the mouse to capture a gentle reflection of the body. |

| **Honeycomb perforations** | Uniform hexagonal cells (≈ 2 mm across) cut into the shell. The holes expose a slightly darker inner layer, giving a **subtle depth** effect. | Light leaks through the holes, creating tiny bright spots when the RGB glow is active. |

The strip emits a **soft, diffuse cyan (#00e5ff) glow** that bleeds into the surrounding matte surface. | In the still photo the glow appears as a **thin luminous line** with a slight halo; a faint reflective sheen on the adjacent matte surface adds realism. |
| **Buttons (5 programmable + DPI switch)** | Slightly concave, **semi‑glossy black** surfaces with a subtle bevel. The programmable buttons are larger (≈ 12 × 8 mm) and have tiny embossed icons (e.g., forward/back arrows). The DPI button is smaller (≈ 6 mm), circular, with a simple “▲” symbol. | The buttons have a **very faint specular highlight** that distinguishes them from the matte body. |

| **Braided paracord cable** | Tightly woven, ~2 mm diameter, matte black with a subtle sheen. The weave shows a repeating **diamond pattern** under close inspection. | The cable has **soft, diffuse reflections** from the fill light, and the individual strands are discernible at macro scale. |
| **Weight & shape cues** | The mouse appears **slim and streamlined**, with a low‑profile silhouette. No visible weight vents or bulky extrusions – the lightweight nature is conveyed through the sleek, tapered lines. | The bottom edge shows a slight **bevel** that catches a faint rim light, reinforcing the ultralight aesthetic. |

## 3. Lighting Setup (Studio)

| Light | Position | Angle | Color / Temp | Intensity / Modifiers |
|-------|----------|-------|--------------|-----------------------|
| **Key Light (softbox)** | Upper‑left, ~45° horizontal, 30° above the mouse. | 45° from camera left, 30° up. | Neutral daylight (≈ 5600 K). | 1.5 stop over ambient; used with a **large (120 × 80 cm) rectangular softbox** to produce soft, wrap‑around shadows. |
| **Fill Light (white reflector / small softbox)** | Right side, opposite the key, at the same height as the mouse. | 30° horizontal from camera right. | Neutral (5600 K) but dimmer (≈ 0.5 stop). | A **diffusion panel** to soften harsh fill and retain the matte finish without creating specular highlights. |
| **Rim Light (thin LED strip or small fresnel)** | Directly behind the mouse, slightly above the plane of the surface, aimed toward the camera. | 0° horizontal, 5° up. | Warm white (≈ 3200 K) to add a subtle separation glow on the edges. | Low intensity (≈ 0.3 stop) to create a thin **highlight halo** around the silhouette. |
| **RGB Accent Light (LED strip)** | Positioned behind the side‑strip region (left side of the mouse) on a small, translucent holder. | 10° behind the mouse’s side, pointing forward. | Pure cyan (#00e5ff) at ~30 % power. | The light is **diffused** through a thin frosted acrylic panel to create a soft, even glow along the side strip without overpowering the matte body. |
| **Reflector (under‑mouse)** | Placed on the table surface, directly beneath the mouse, angled slightly upward. | 0° vertical. | Neutral white. | A **white matte board** (or a small 30 × 30 cm diffusion panel) to catch a faint **bottom‑up reflection** of the mouse’s underside, giving a subtle “floating” feel. |
| **Background Light (optional)** | Centered behind the mouse, a few inches from the backdrop. | Straight on, slightly above the mouse. | Dark teal (#001f2a) at low intensity to give a faint gradient glow on the backdrop. | Adjustable; used only if the background appears too flat. |

**Resulting Lighting Mood**  
- **Key + Fill** produce a balanced, low‑contrast illumination that preserves the matte texture and the fine perforations.  
- **Rim light** separates the mouse cleanly from the dark background, preventing it from merging into the shadows.  
- **Cyan accent light** makes the side‑strip glow look organic and not “photoshopped”.  
- **Reflector** adds a faint, pleasing “ground reflection” without breaking the sleek silhouette.

## 4. Camera Angle & Lens

| Parameter | Setting |
|-----------|----------|
| **Camera Position** | 3‑Quarter view: the camera is **slightly above the mouse (≈ 20° upward tilt)** and angled **~30° to the left of the mouse’s forward axis**. This shows the front USB‑C port, the top buttons, and the side RGB strip simultaneously. |
| **Lens** | **Macro prime (90 mm‑105 mm)** on a full‑frame camera, **f/8 – f/11** for a deep‑enough depth of field to keep the whole mouse sharp while still maintaining a gentle background blur. |
| **Focus** | Centered on the **mid‑point of the mouse** (roughly the middle of the body), with the depth of field extending just enough to keep the side‑strip glow and the front port in focus. |
| **Composition Rule** | Apply the **rule of thirds** – place the mouse’s center slightly off‑center (≈ ⅓ from the left edge) to leave breathing room for the coiled cable and a small “logo” area (if a brand mark is desired) on the right. |
| **Exposure** | ISO 100, shutter speed 1/200 s (to freeze any subtle movement of the cable or the LED glow), white balance set to **5500 K** (slightly cooler than the key light for a crisp look). |
| **Post‑Processing (if needed)** | Slight **clarity** increase to bring out the honeycomb texture, **localized contrast** on the side‑strip to emphasize the cyan glow, and a **soft vignette** to keep the focus on the mouse. |

## 5. Background & Setting

- **Cyclorama (seamless paper or vinyl)** that curves smoothly from the table surface to the back wall. Color: **deep charcoal (#1a1a1f)** at the base, fading to **near‑black (#0d0d10)** at the top.
- **Subtle texture** (optional): a very faint **hex‑grid or carbon‑fiber pattern** embossed into the backdrop at ≤ 5 % opacity, echoing the mouse’s honeycomb design without overpowering.
- **No distracting elements** – only the mouse and its coiled cable appear in the foreground; the background remains clean and uncluttered.
- **Atmosphere**: the scene feels **clean, technical, and premium**, reminiscent of high‑end PC‑hardware marketing (e.g., ASUS ROG, Razer, Logitech G).

1. **For a photographer:** hand the “Visual Layout” and “Lighting Setup” sections as a shot brief; they dictate where to position lights, the camera, and the mouse.
2. **For a CGI artist:** translate each “Materials & Textures” bullet into shader inputs (e.g., roughness 0.85, metalness 0.0 for the body; emissive intensity 0.4 for the cyan strip). Use the same lighting rig virtually.

The result should be a **clean, high‑impact product image** that instantly communicates “premium”, “ultralight”, and “gaming‑ready,” while highlighting the unique honeycomb design and RGB accent.
