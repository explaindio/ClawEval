### 🇪🇸 Cultural Adaptation Notes (Spanish)

**Cloud9/Cloud Nine:**
*   **Adaptation:** Translated the concept of "peak experience" rather than the literal name.
*   **Note:** *Nuestra nueva plataforma Cloud9* (Our new Cloud9 platform) was translated as *Nuestra nueva plataforma* (Our new platform), with the implied meaning of "peak performance" carried by the surrounding text.

**Home Run:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *éxito rotundo* (resounding success).
*   **Note:** This phrase conveys the same meaning of a major, undeniable success in a professional context.

**Having your cake and eating it too:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *tener lo mejor de dos mundos* (to have the best of two worlds).
*   **Note:** This is the standard Spanish equivalent for achieving a perfect, desirable balance.

**Gone the whole nine yards:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *hemos puesto todo nuestro empeño* (we have put all our effort/dedication).
*   **Note:** This captures the meaning of putting maximum effort or going above and beyond.

**Your grandmother could use it:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *Incluso tu abuela podría usarla* (Even your grandmother could use it).
*   **Note:** While the literal translation is used, the context implies that the platform is so simple that even someone unfamiliar with technology can use it, which is the intended meaning.

**Rain or shine:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *en todo momento* (at all times).
*   **Note:** This conveys the unconditional nature of the availability (24/7).

**Bee's knees:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *lo máximo* (the maximum/the best).
*   **Note:** This is a common, enthusiastic way to describe something as excellent or top-tier in Latin American Spanish.

**Whistling Dixie:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *no estamos bromeando* (we are not joking).
*   **Note:** This conveys the seriousness and sincerity of the claim, meaning the company is not exaggerating.

**Black Friday:**
*   **Preserved:** The term *Black Friday* is widely adopted and understood in Spanish-speaking commerce.
*   **Note:** The term is kept as it is a global commercial event.

**Early-bird:**
*   **Adapted:** Translated as *precios de preventa* (pre-sale prices).
*   **Note:** This is the most accurate commercial translation for early-bird pricing.

## 🇯🇵 Japanese Translation

**「弊社の新しいCloud9プラットフォームは、まさに大成功です。それは、最高の状態を両立させるようなものです。ユーザーフレンドリーにするため、私たちは全力を尽くしました。おばあ様でも簡単に使えますよ！年中無休でご利用いただけます。これは最高だと確信しており、本気で言っているのです。早期割引価格はブラックフライデー前にご登録ください。」**

***

### 🇯🇵 Cultural Adaptation Notes (Japanese)

**Cloud9/Cloud Nine:**
*   **Adaptation:** Translated the concept of "peak experience" using the phrase *最高の状態* (saikō no jōtai - the best state/condition).
*   **Note:** While *Cloud Nine* is sometimes used in Japanese, translating the concept ensures the meaning of ultimate satisfaction is clear to a broader audience.

**Home Run:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *まさに大成功です* (masani daiseikō desu - it is truly a great success).
*   **Note:** This is the standard, formal way to describe a major, undeniable success in Japanese business language.

**Having your cake and eating it too:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *最高の状態を両立させるようなものです* (saikō no jōtai o ryōritsu saseru yō na mono desu - it is like achieving the best state simultaneously).
*   **Note:** This captures the meaning of achieving a perfect balance or ideal situation.

**Gone the whole nine yards:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *私たちは全力を尽くしました* (watashitachi wa zenryoku o tsukushimashita - we put forth our full effort).
*   **Note:** This is the standard, formal way to express maximum effort or dedication in Japanese.

**Your grandmother could use it:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *おばあ様でも簡単に使えますよ* (obā-sama demo kantan ni tsukaemasu yo - even your grandmother can use it easily).
*   **Note:** The literal translation is used, but the context implies that the platform is extremely intuitive and simple, which is the intended meaning.

**Rain or shine:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *年中無休でご利用いただけます* (nenjū mukyū de go-riyō itadakemasu - it can be used year-round without holidays).
*   **Note:** This is the standard Japanese business phrase for 24/7 availability, conveying unconditional service.

**Bee's knees:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *これは最高だと確信しており* (kore wa saikō da to kakushin shite ori - we are convinced this is the best).
*   **Note:** *最高* (saikō) is the most common and powerful way to describe something as excellent or top-tier.

**Whistling Dixie:**
*   **Replaced:** The idiom was replaced with *本気で言っているのです* (honki de itte iru no desu - we are saying it seriously).
*   **Note:** This conveys the sincerity and seriousness of the claim, meaning the company is not exaggerating.

**Black Friday:**
*   **Preserved:** The term *ブラックフライデー* (Burakku Furai Dē) is widely adopted in Japanese commerce.
*   **Note:** The term is kept as it is a global commercial event.

**Early-bird:**
*   **Adapted:** Translated as *早期割引価格* (sōki waribiki kakaku - early discount price).
*   **Note:** This is the most accurate commercial translation for early-bird pricing.
