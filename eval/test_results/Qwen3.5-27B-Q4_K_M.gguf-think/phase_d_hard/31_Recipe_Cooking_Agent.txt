- Guest D: Low-sodium diet (under 500mg sodium per serving)

Create a 3-course menu. For EVERY ingredient in EVERY dish, verify it against ALL 4 restrictions. If an ingredient could cause confusion (e.g., "natural flavors" which might contain gluten), flag it. Show the verification matrix.

# 3-Course Dinner Menu: "The Safe Harvest"

**Guest Count:** 4
**Dietary Profile:** Vegan, Celiac (Strict GF), Severe Tree Nut Allergy, Low-Sodium (<500mg/serving).

### ⚠️ THE TRAP ALERT
**The Trap Ingredient:** **Regular Soy Sauce**
**Why it's a trap:** It is a staple in plant-based cooking for umami flavor and appears vegan-friendly. However, traditional soy sauce is brewed with **wheat** (violating Guest B's Celiac restriction) and is extremely **high in sodium** (violating Guest D's restriction).
**The Solution:** I have excluded soy sauce entirely. Flavor is derived from fresh herbs, citrus, and low-sodium vegetable broth to ensure safety for all guests.

### Final Safety Checks & Flags
1.  **Broth:** Do not use bouillon cubes. They often contain high sodium and hidden gluten. Use a carton labeled "Certified Gluten-Free" and "Low Sodium."
2.  **Spices:** Do not use pre-mixed spice blends (e.g., "Taco Seasoning"). These often contain wheat flour or high sodium. Use single-ingredient spices.
3.  **Maple Syrup:** Do not use "Honey" or "Agave Blend" (some agave blends have additives). Use 100% Pure Maple Syrup.
4.  **Tofu:** While soy is a legume (safe for Guest C), ensure the brand does not process tree nuts in the same facility to prevent cross-contamination.
5.  **Quinoa:** Ensure the bag states "Certified Gluten-Free." Standard quinoa is often processed in facilities with wheat.
