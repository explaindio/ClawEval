## PROMPT:
Analyze this clinical trial data. WARNING: The obvious conclusion from the aggregate data is WRONG. You must analyze the subgroups to find the truth. This is a real statistical phenomenon.

Overall Results:
- Treatment A: 100 patients, 40 recovered (40%)
- Treatment B: 100 patients, 50 recovered (50%)

Subgroup breakdown:
- Mild cases with Treatment A: 10 patients, 8 recovered (80%)
- Mild cases with Treatment B: 60 patients, 45 recovered (75%)
- Severe cases with Treatment A: 90 patients, 32 recovered (35.6%)
- Severe cases with Treatment B: 40 patients, 5 recovered (12.5%)

Questions:
1. Based on aggregate data alone, which treatment appears better?
2. When you look at subgroups, which treatment is actually better for mild cases?
3. Which treatment is actually better for severe cases?
4. Explain the paradox — why does the aggregate data mislead?

## RESPONSE:
Here is the analysis of the clinical trial data based on your requirements.

### 1. Based on aggregate data alone, which treatment appears better?
Based on the aggregate data, **Treatment B** appears better.
*   Treatment A Recovery Rate: 40%

*   **Conclusion:** Treatment B shows a 10 percentage point advantage in the overall summary.

### 2. When you look at subgroups, which treatment is actually better for mild cases?
**Treatment A** is better for mild cases.
*   Treatment A (Mild): 80% recovery rate.
*   Treatment B (Mild): 75% recovery rate.
*   **Conclusion:** Treatment A outperforms Treatment B by 5 percentage points in mild cases.

### 3. Which treatment is actually better for severe cases?
**Treatment A** is better for severe cases.
*   Treatment A (Severe): 35.6% recovery rate.
*   Treatment B (Severe): 12.5% recovery rate.
*   **Conclusion:** Treatment A outperforms Treatment B by 23.1 percentage points in severe cases.

### 4. Explain the paradox — why does the aggregate data mislead?
The aggregate data is misleading due to a **confounding variable**: the severity of the illness.

*   **Treatment B** was administered primarily to patients with **mild cases** (60 out of 100 patients). Because mild cases have a naturally higher recovery rate regardless of treatment, Treatment B's overall success rate was artificially inflated.
*   **Treatment A** was administered primarily to patients with **severe cases** (90 out of 100 patients). Because severe cases are much harder to treat and have a naturally lower recovery rate, Treatment A's overall success rate was artificially suppressed.

Even though Treatment A is more effective in *both* categories, it was tested on a much sicker population, dragging down its aggregate average.

### 5. What is the name of this statistical phenomenon?
This is known as **Simpson's Paradox**.
It occurs when a trend appears in different groups of data but disappears or reverses when these groups are combined.

### 6. What is your final recommendation and why?
**Recommendation:** **Treatment A** should be the standard of care.

**Reasoning:**
When controlling for the severity of the illness, Treatment A demonstrates superior efficacy in **both** mild and severe patient populations. Relying on the aggregate data would lead to the selection of an inferior treatment (Treatment B) simply because it was tested on a healthier cohort. To ensure the best outcomes for patients regardless of their condition severity, Treatment A is the statistically superior choice.
