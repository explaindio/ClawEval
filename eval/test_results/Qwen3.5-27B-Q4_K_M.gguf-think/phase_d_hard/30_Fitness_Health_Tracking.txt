*   **Elevated Resting Heart Rate (RHR):** In Week 1, RHR climbed steadily from 58 to 67 bpm. A sustained rise of >5-10 bpm is a primary indicator of autonomic nervous system stress and insufficient recovery.

#### 2. The Data Inconsistency
**The Caloric Intake vs. Weight Loss Discrepancy.**
The user claims to consume **3,500 calories/day** but has lost **4 lbs in 2 weeks**.
*   **The Math:** To lose 4 lbs of body mass in 14 days, a deficit of approximately 14,000 calories is required (3,500 calories/lb). This equals a daily deficit of ~1,000 calories.
*   **The Contradiction:** A 3,500 calorie intake is typically a surplus for most individuals. To lose weight while eating 3,500 calories, the user's Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) would need to be 4,500+ calories/day. This is elite-athlete territory (e.g., Tour de France cyclists).
*   **The Reality:** It is highly probable the user is **underreporting food intake** (likely eating 2,000–2,500 calories) OR the weight loss is not fat, but **muscle tissue and water** due to a catabolic state caused by high cortisol levels from overtraining and sleep deprivation.
