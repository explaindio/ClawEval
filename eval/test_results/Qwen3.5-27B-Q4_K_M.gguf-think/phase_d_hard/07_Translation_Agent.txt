### Japanese Translation

「新しい Cloud9 ホスティングは、あなたのサイトを『雲の上』に浮かべます！良い隣人のように、ServerFarm はいつもそばにいます。卵を一つのカゴに盛るな——マルチリージョンフェイルオーバーにより、データは常に安全です。Usain Bolt さんですら嫉妬するスピードで、ページは『supercalifragilisticexpialidocious』と言える前に読み込まれます。」

[TRANSLATOR NOTE: PUN - "Cloud9/cloud nine"]
**Adaptation:** Adapted. The Japanese idiom for extreme happiness is "雲の上" (kumo no ue - on the clouds) or "天国" (tenkoku - heaven), but it does not include the number "nine". To maintain the brand name "Cloud9" (クラウド9), I translated the idiom to "雲の上" to keep the "cloud" imagery, though the specific numerical pun is lost. This prioritizes brand recognition over the wordplay.

[TRANSLATOR NOTE: CULTURE-SPECIFIC REFERENCE - "Like a good neighbor"]
**Adaptation:** Translated literally ("良い隣人のように"). This references the State Farm slogan. Since State Farm is not a major brand in Japan, the cultural reference will likely be missed. I kept the literal translation to preserve the tone of reliability and neighborliness, acknowledging that the specific brand parody is US-centric.

[TRANSLATOR NOTE: IDIOM - "Eggs in one basket"]
**Adaptation:** Adapted. The standard Japanese equivalent is "卵を一つのカゴに盛るな" (Tamago o hitotsu no kago ni moruna). This is a direct translation of the English idiom and is widely understood in Japanese business contexts to mean avoiding concentrated risk.

[TRANSLATOR NOTE: CULTURE-SPECIFIC REFERENCE - Usain Bolt]
**Adaptation:** Preserved. Usain Bolt is well-known in Japan as the world's fastest sprinter. His name is kept in Katakana (Usain Bolt) to ensure immediate recognition of the speed reference.

[TRANSLATOR NOTE: CULTURE-SPECIFIC REFERENCE - "supercalifragilisticexpialidocious"]
**Adaptation:** Preserved. While the Japanese dub of *Mary Poppins* uses a translated version of the song, the original English word is iconic enough among pop-culture enthusiasts to convey the "long word" joke. Keeping the English word emphasizes the difficulty of saying it quickly, reinforcing the speed claim.
