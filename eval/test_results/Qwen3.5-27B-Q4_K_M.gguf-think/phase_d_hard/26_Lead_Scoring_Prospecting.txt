Score each 1-100, identify the tire-kicker, rank them, and explain your reasoning.

### Detailed Reasoning

#### 1. Lead 4 (Score: 95) - The Buyer
*   **Why #1:** This lead checks every box of the BANT framework (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline).
*   **Signals:** They are asking about **SOC 2 compliance** (security readiness) and **volume discounts** (intent to scale). Crucially, they mentioned **Q2 budget allocation**.
*   **Urgency:** The activity is fresh (yesterday), and the questions are transactional, not educational. They are ready to move to procurement.
*   **Risk:** Procurement managers can be gatekeepers, but when they ask about volume discounts and compliance, they are usually the ones facilitating the final signature.

#### 2. Lead 3 (Score: 85) - The Champion
*   **Why #2:** This is the strongest *product* fit.
*   **Signals:** Building a **POC (Proof of Concept)** is the strongest indicator of intent after a demo. Inviting **3 teammates** signals potential adoption and internal advocacy. Asking about **SSO** means they are planning for enterprise integration.
*   **Risk:** They are a Developer, not necessarily the budget holder. They need to convince the decision-makers (like Lead 4). However, the deal is already in motion.

#### 3. Lead 1 (Score: 35) - The Tire-Kicker (The Trap)
*   **Why #3:** This lead is the specific warning in your prompt.
*   **Signals:** **18 months** of activity without a demo is a massive red flag. In SaaS, 18 months is an eternity; the market moves too fast.
*   **The Trap:** They are consuming content to feel productive without committing. They explicitly told sales "no timeline."
*   **Strategy:** Do not waste sales resources here. Move them to a nurture stream. They are likely researching for a future project or gathering data for a competitor analysis.

#### 4. Lead 5 (Score: 20) - The Ghost
*   **Why #4:** They showed initial interest but lost momentum.
*   **Signals:** 3 days of activity followed by **6 weeks of silence** indicates they either found a solution elsewhere, got distracted, or realized the product wasn't a fit.
*   **Strategy:** A re-engagement email is worth one attempt, but they are likely lost.

#### 5. Lead 2 (Score: 10) - The Stale Lead
*   **Why #5:** This lead is effectively dead.
*   **Signals:** Visited pricing **3 months ago** with no follow-up.
*   **Strategy:** Do not contact. This data is too old to be actionable.

### Summary of Strategy
*   **Prioritize Lead 4 immediately.** They have the budget and the timeline.
*   **Support Lead 3.** Help them build their case so they can sell internally to the budget holder.
*   **Ignore Lead 1.** Do not let their high content consumption fool you; they are not buying.
*   **Archive Leads 2 & 5.** They are not currently viable opportunities.
