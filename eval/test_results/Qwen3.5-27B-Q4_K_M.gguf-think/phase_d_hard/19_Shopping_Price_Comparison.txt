Compare these 4 products. WARNING: The cheapest option is NOT the best value when you calculate total cost of ownership. Show your math.

Product A — Laptop Alpha: $899

- Required accessories: $150 dock, $80 adapter

Product B — Laptop Beta: $1,299
- 16GB RAM, 512GB SSD (user upgradable)
- Battery: 10 hours, replaceable ($80)
- Expected lifespan: 5 years, extended warranty $100/year
- All ports included, no accessories needed

Product C — Laptop Gamma: $749
- 8GB RAM, 128GB SSD
- Battery: 4 hours, non-replaceable
- Expected lifespan: 2 years, no warranty

## RESPONSE:
### Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Analysis (5-Year Horizon)

To determine the true value, we must calculate the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**. This includes the initial purchase price, required accessories, battery replacements, warranty fees, and the cost of replacing the laptop if its lifespan is shorter than 5 years.

**Key Assumptions for Calculation:**
1.  **Timeframe:** 5 Years.
2.  **Laptop Replacement:** If a laptop's expected lifespan is less than 5 years, a new unit must be purchased to cover the remaining time.
3.  **Accessories:** Required accessories (docks, adapters, external SSDs) are purchased with **each** new laptop unit to ensure compatibility and functionality.
4.  **Battery Replacement:** Lithium-ion batteries typically degrade after 2–3 years. We assume **one battery replacement** is required for any unit lasting 3+ years to maintain performance.
5.  **Warranty:** Warranty costs are included as a necessary expense to protect the investment over the lifespan.

---

### 1. Product A — Laptop Alpha
*   **Lifespan:** 3 Years (Requires 2 units to cover 5 years).
*   **Accessories:** $150 (Dock) + $80 (Adapter) = **$230 per unit**.
*   **Battery:** Replacement needed for Unit 1 (3-year life). Unit 2 (2-year life) does not need replacement.

**Math:**
*   **Unit 1 (Years 1–3):** $899 (Laptop) + $230 (Accessories) + $200 (Battery) = **$1,329**
*   **Unit 2 (Years 4–5):** $899 (Laptop) + $230 (Accessories) = **$1,129**
*   **Total TCO:** $1,329 + $1,129 = **$2,458**

### 2. Product B — Laptop Beta
*   **Lifespan:** 5 Years (Requires 1 unit).
*   **Accessories:** None required (**$0**).
*   **Battery:** Replacement needed for Unit 1 (5-year life).
*   **Warranty:** $100/year × 5 years = **$500**.

**Math:**
*   **Unit 1 (Years 1–5):** $1,299 (Laptop) + $0 (Accessories) + $500 (Warranty) + $80 (Battery) = **$1,879**
*   **Total TCO:** **$1,879**

### 3. Product C — Laptop Gamma
*   **Lifespan:** 2 Years (Requires 3 units to cover 5 years).
*   **Accessories:** $200 (SSD) + $150 (Dock) + $80 (Adapter) = **$430 per unit**.
*   **Battery:** Non-replaceable (Cost included in lifespan limit).

**Math:**
*   **Unit 1 (Years 1–2):** $749 (Laptop) + $430 (Accessories) = **$1,179**
*   **Unit 2 (Years 3–4):** $749 (Laptop) + $430 (Accessories) = **$1,179**
*   **Unit 3 (Year 5):** $749 (Laptop) + $430 (Accessories) = **$1,179**
*   **Total TCO:** $1,179 × 3 = **$3,537**

### 4. Product D — Laptop Delta
*   **Lifespan:** 4 Years (Requires 2 units to cover 5 years).
*   **Accessories:** $80 (Adapter) = **$80 per unit**.
*   **Battery:** Replacement needed for Unit 1 (4-year life). Unit 2 (1-year life) does not need replacement.

**Math:**
