Q: What are the different pricing tiers available for CloudSync Pro?
A: CloudSync Pro is available in three tiers: Free, Pro ($9.99/mo), and Enterprise ($29.99/mo).

Q: How much storage is included with the Free tier?
A: While one section of the documentation claims all tiers include unlimited storage, the specific limitation for the Free tier is 5GB. The "unlimited storage" feature applies only to the paid Pro and Enterprise tiers.

Q: What is the maximum file size I can upload?
A: Free users can upload files up to 2GB, whereas paid users (Pro and Enterprise) have a maximum file size limit of 10GB.

Q: Which operating systems and devices are supported?
A: The desktop service supports Windows, Mac, and Linux. Currently, mobile apps are available exclusively for iOS, though Android support is scheduled for release in Q2 2025.

Q: How secure are my files on CloudSync Pro?
A: All files are secured using AES-256 encryption both at rest and while in transit.

Q: What is the data retention policy for deleted files?
A: The standard retention period is 30 days for most users. However, this contradicts the specific allowance for Enterprise users, who benefit from an extended recovery window of up to 90 days.

Q: Does CloudSync Pro offer an uptime guarantee?
A: The documentation contains conflicting information regarding the SLA. While it states there is a 99.9% uptime SLA for all tiers, it explicitly notes that Free tier users are not covered. Therefore, the 99.9% uptime guarantee applies only to paid Pro and Enterprise subscribers.

Q: Can I recover files on the Free plan?
A: Yes, Free tier users can recover deleted files for up to 30 days, provided the files are within the 5GB storage limit and 2GB file size limit.

**Contradictions Found:**

1.  **Storage Limits:** The documentation states "All tiers include unlimited storage" but later specifies that "The Free tier is limited to 5GB of storage."
2.  **SLA Coverage:** The text claims the "uptime SLA is 99.9% for all tiers," but immediately contradicts this by stating "Free tier users are not covered by the SLA."
3.  **File Recovery Period:** The documentation asserts "Deleted files are recoverable for 30 days" as a general rule, which is contradicted by the statement that "Enterprise users can recover files for up to 90 days."
