## 2. Lighting Setup (how to light the scene)

| Light | Position / Angle | Modifiers / Color | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Key light (softbox)** | **Left‑front, 45° from camera**, ~1.2 m away. | Large 90 × 120 cm softbox, diffusion panel (1.5 stop). Warm‑white (5600 K) or neutral daylight. | Reveals texture of the matte body, gives gentle contrast on the honeycomb cells. |
| **Fill light** | **Right‑front, 30° from camera**, lower intensity (≈½ the key). | Small 60 × 60 cm softbox or a 5‑in‑1 reflector on a stand. Neutral white. | Lifts shadows on the left side and the RGB strip, prevents harsh black areas. |
| **Rim / hair light** | **Behind the mouse, 10–15° above the desk**, pointing toward the camera. | LED strip (or a focusing flash) with a **cyan gel** (or RGB‑controlled to cyan). | Creates a thin rim of cyan light that enhances the RGB strip glow, adds a halo around the silhouette. |
| **Accent light for cable** | **Low angle, just behind the cable**, pointing at the braided texture. | Small LED panel with a **soft diffusion** and a **cyan tint** (≈10 % intensity). | Highlights the paracord weave and the neon threads. |
| **Background wash** | **Behind the whole setup**, about 1 m away, angled slightly downward. | Large softbox or a **gradient LED panel** (dark charcoal to black).  No direct light on the background – keep it matte and unobtrusive. | Provides a clean, dark backdrop that isolates the mouse. |
| **Optional reflector** | A **large black/white reversible reflector** placed at **45° opposite the key light**. | White side facing the mouse for fill, black side for deepening shadows if you want more contrast. | Control the mood (high‑contrast vs. soft). |

*All lights should be **diffused** to avoid harsh specular hotspots on the matte surface.  Use a **polarizing filter** on the lens if any stray reflections appear.*

## 3. Camera Angle & Lens

| Parameter | Setting / Choice |
|---|---|
| **Camera** | Full‑frame mirrorless (e.g., Sony A7R V, Canon EOS R5) or high‑resolution DSLR. |
| **Lens** | **85 mm prime** (or 90 mm macro) at **f/2.8 – f/4**.  Gives a flattering perspective with gentle background compression. |
| **Focus** | **Single‑point AF** locked on the **front edge of the USB‑C port** (the most detailed area).  If you want the whole mouse sharp, use **focus stacking**: 3‑5 shots at f/4, f/5.6, f/8. |
| **Perspective** | **3/4 view** – camera positioned **slightly above eye‑level (≈10° down‑tilt)** and **15–20° to the right** of the mouse’s centerline.  This angle shows the **top surface (honeycomb, DPI button, scroll wheel), the left side (RGB strip), and the front (USB‑C)** all in one frame. |
| **Depth of field** | **Shallow but controlled**: front edge of mouse is sharp, the rear (palm rest) is slightly softer (≈1/3 of the frame out of focus). |
| **Tripod & Release** | Use a sturdy tripod; trigger via remote or self‑timer to eliminate vibration. |
| **Exposure** | ISO 100, **1/125 s**, aperture as above.  Adjust flash power to achieve a **guide number of 16–18** at the subject distance. |

No patterns, logos, or distracting elements.  A **very faint, circular cyan glow** (matching the RGB strip) can be projected on the wall for continuity, but keep it subtle to avoid distraction. |

| **Props (optional)** | A **single sleek USB‑C cable** (the same braided paracord) coiled neatly next to the mouse, or a **minimalist gaming keyboard** (same matte black finish) in the far background, out of focus, to suggest a setup.  Keep the scene **clean**; the mouse is the hero. |

## 6. Post‑Processing Hints (for the final image)

| Adjustment | Goal |
|---|---|
| **Exposure & contrast** | Slightly lift the mid‑tones to keep the matte surface from looking flat; add a touch of contrast to define the honeycomb edges. |
| **Color grading** | Boost the **cyan** channel slightly (≈5 %) to make the RGB strip pop; keep the rest of the image neutral (no warm or cool cast). |
| **Vignette** | Add a very subtle vignette (radius 80 %, intensity 10 %) to draw the eye to the center. |
| **Noise reduction** | Apply light luminance NR to keep the micro‑texture smooth while preserving fine detail. |
| **Highlight recovery** | Recover any blown‑out rim‑light reflection on the side strip. |
| **Shadow softening** | Keep the drop shadow under the mouse soft and low‑opacity for a natural look. |
| **Sharpening** | Use **selective sharpening** on the honeycomb cells, USB‑C port, and cable braid; avoid over‑sharpening the matte body to prevent artifacts. |
