Here’s an **extremely detailed** breakdown of what a **professional product photo** for your **premium wireless gaming mouse** should include, structured for an **image prompt** (e.g., for AI generation, photography direction, or 3D rendering). This covers **composition, materials, lighting, angles, and background** to maximize appeal for marketing or e-commerce.

---

### **1. Visual Layout & Composition**
#### **Primary Focus: The Mouse Itself**
- **Central Placement**: The mouse should dominate the frame (~70-80% of the image), with negative space around it to emphasize its sleek design.
- **Orientation**:
  - **Side View (45° Angle)**: Best for showcasing the **RGB side strip**, **honeycomb pattern**, and **USB-C port**. The mouse should be angled slightly upward (10-15°) to avoid flatness.
  - **Top-Down View (Optional)**: For a secondary shot to highlight the **5 programmable buttons**, **DPI switch**, and **ultralight honeycomb shell**.
  - **Front View (Straight-On)**: To emphasize the **USB-C port** and **braided paracord** (detached, coiled neatly beside the mouse).

#### **Key Features to Highlight Visually**
- **RGB Side Strip**:
  - **Cyan glow** should be **vibrant but not overpowering** (subtle pulse or static glow, not flashing).
  - The strip should run **vertically along the left or right side** (whichever looks more dynamic; left is common for right-handed users).
  - **Light bleed**: Slight diffusion around the edges to imply a soft, premium LED quality.
- **Honeycomb Perforated Shell**:
  - **Depth and texture**: The perforations should be **sharp, hexagonal, and evenly spaced**, with **visible light filtering through** (backlit faintly to show translucency).
  - **Material**: Appears **matte but slightly reflective** (like sanded aluminum or high-end plastic).
- **USB-C Port**:
  - **Front-facing**, centered or slightly offset to the right.
  - **Metal finish** (brushed or polished) contrasting with the matte body.
  - **Cable**: Detached **braided paracord** (black or matching color) coiled **loosely** beside the mouse (not tangled).
- **Programmable Buttons**:
  - **5 buttons total**: 2 side buttons (symmetrical), 1 top DPI switch, and 2 thumb buttons (if applicable).
  - **Textured surface**: Slightly **grip-friendly** (soft rubber or textured plastic) but still sleek.
  - **DPI Switch**: Should look **tactile and premium** (e.g., a small, raised button or slider).
- **Weight & Size Cues**:
  - **No visible "heaviness"**: The mouse should look **floating** due to its ultralight (67g) design.
  - **Size reference**: Optional **subtle scale** (e.g., a **1€ coin** placed beside it in a secondary shot for context).

### **3. Lighting Setup**
#### **Primary Lighting (Key Light)**
- **Type**: **Softbox or LED panel** (5000K–6500K, cool white).
- **Position**: **45° angle from the front-left** (for a side view) or **top-left** (for a top-down view).
- **Intensity**: **80-90% power** to avoid harsh shadows but retain depth.
- **Effect**:
  - **Subtle catchlights** on the honeycomb perforations.
  - **Soft gradient** on the mouse body (darker on the bottom, lighter on the top).
  - **RGB glow** should be **evenly diffused** (not spotty).

#### **Secondary Lighting (Fill Light)**
- **Type**: **Diffused LED strip or second softbox**.
- **Position**: **Opposite the key light** (45° from the front-right).
- **Intensity**: **30-50% power** to **minimize shadows** but keep contrast.
- **Effect**:
  - **Even illumination** on the underside of the mouse.
  - **No dark crevices** (especially around the USB-C port).

#### **Backlight (Optional for Depth)**
- **Type**: **Neutral-colored LED strip** (placed behind the mouse).
- **Intensity**: **Low (10-20%)** to create a **hairlight effect**.
- **Effect**:
  - **Gentle glow** through the honeycomb shell.
  - **Separates the mouse from the background** slightly.

#### **RGB Lighting Control**
- The **cyan RGB strip** should be set to:
  - **Static glow** (not flashing) at **~50% brightness**.
  - **Slight color temperature shift** (e.g., cyan with a **cool blue undertone** for premium feel).

### **4. Camera Angle & Focus**
#### **Primary Shot: Side View (45° Angle)**
- **Camera Height**: **Slightly above the mouse** (as if looking down at it from a 10° angle).
- **Depth of Field**:
  - **Shallow focus** (f/2.8–f/4) to **blur the background** and keep the mouse sharp.
  - **Critical focus** on:
    - The **RGB strip**.
    - The **USB-C port**.
    - The **honeycomb texture**.
- **Perspective**:
  - **Barrel distortion minimized** (use a **wide-angle lens with distortion correction** if needed).
  - **No fisheye effect**—keep it **realistic and professional**.

#### **Secondary Shots (Optional)**
1. **Top-Down View**:
   - **Camera directly above**, slight tilt to show **button layout**.
   - **RGB strip visible** on the side.
2. **Front View**:
   - **Straight-on**, emphasizing the **USB-C port** and **paracord**.
3. **In-Use Shot (Lifestyle)**:
   - **Hand holding the mouse** (right-hand grip, fingers on buttons).
   - **RGB glow visible** on the side.
   - **Paracord coiled neatly** on a desk.

### **6. Post-Processing (For Digital/Photography)**
- **Color Grading**:
  - **Cool tones** (slight blue tint) to enhance the **premium tech feel**.
  - **RGB glow** should remain **vibrant but not oversaturated**.
- **Sharpening**:
  - **Selective sharpening** on the **honeycomb texture** and **button edges**.
- **Noise Reduction**:
  - **Minimal noise** (especially in shadows).
- **Final Touches**:
  - **Subtle vignette** (darkened edges) to draw eyes to the mouse.
  - **No watermarks or logos** (unless branding is intentional).

### **8. Additional Notes for Photographers**
- **Avoid**:
  - **Direct sunlight** (creates harsh shadows).
  - **Over-saturated RGB** (should look **subtle and premium**).

- **Use**:
  - **Light tent** for soft, even lighting.
  - **Color checker** to ensure accurate RGB reproduction.
  - **Macro lens** (e.g., 100mm) for **ultra-sharp details**.
