## PROMPT:
Handle this support conversation. The customer has a LEGITIMATE complaint but is making 2 unreasonable demands. You must identify which demands are reasonable vs unreasonable WITHOUT making the customer feel dismissed.

Customer: "I've been a premium subscriber for 4 years paying $99/month. Three times this month, I lost work because your auto-save failed. I want:
1. A full refund for this month ($99)
2. Free premium for the next 12 months as compensation
3. A personal guarantee from your CEO that this won't happen again
4. My files recovered from before the auto-save failures
5. The developer who wrote the auto-save code to be fired

I've already posted on Twitter about this and I'm considering legal action."

Respond professionally. You CAN offer: refund for current month, 1-3 months credit, priority support, escalation to engineering. You CANNOT promise: firing employees, CEO personal guarantees, or more than 3 months free. File recovery may or may not be possible.

## RESPONSE:
Okay, here's a response designed to address the customer's concerns, validate their frustration, identify the reasonable/unreasonable requests, and offer a constructive path forward. It aims for empathy and de-escalation while staying within the boundaries of what we *can* do.

```
Subject: Regarding Your Recent Auto-Save Issues - [Account Name/Number]

Dear [Customer Name],

Thank you for reaching out to us. I understand how incredibly frustrating it must be to experience data loss, especially when it impacts your work and you rely on our service as a premium subscriber for four years. Losing work three times in a month is absolutely unacceptable, and I sincerely apologize for the disruption and stress this has caused you.  We take data integrity *very* seriously, and I want to assure you we're committed to resolving this for you.

I've reviewed your account and the reports of the auto-save failures.  I can absolutely process a **full refund for your current month's subscription of $99**.  You deserve that, and I'm initiating that process now.

Regarding your other requests, I want to address each one individually:

*   **Free Premium for the next 12 months:** While I can't offer a full 12 months of free premium at this time, I *can* offer you **three months of complimentary premium access** as a gesture of our apology and to demonstrate our commitment to regaining your trust.  We value your long-term loyalty and want to ensure you continue to have a positive experience.
*   **Personal Guarantee from our CEO:** I understand your desire for reassurance at the highest level. While a personal guarantee from our CEO isn’t something we can provide, I can assure you that this issue is being escalated directly to our engineering leadership team. They are aware of the situation and are prioritizing a thorough investigation.
*   **File Recovery:**  I completely understand the importance of recovering your lost files. Our team will do everything technically possible to restore your data from backups. I'm immediately submitting a request to our data recovery specialists, and they will contact you directly within [Timeframe - e.g., 24-48 hours] to assess the feasibility of recovery.  Please understand that successful recovery isn't always guaranteed, but we will exhaust all available options.
*   **Firing the Developer:** I appreciate your frustration, but personnel decisions are confidential and not something we can discuss or act upon based on individual incidents. Our focus is on identifying the root cause of the issue and implementing a solution to prevent it from happening again.

To further assist you, I'm also assigning your account **priority support**. This means you'll have a dedicated point of contact and faster response times for any future issues.  

I also see you’ve mentioned this on Twitter, and we are monitoring the conversation there.  We want to resolve this directly with you and avoid any further public discussion. 

Regarding your consideration of legal action, I understand your right to explore all options. However, I truly believe we can reach a satisfactory resolution through direct communication and the steps I've outlined above.

Please let me know if you have any questions or would like to discuss this further. I’m here to help.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Support Team Name]
```

**Breakdown of why this works & identification of requests:**

*   **Validates Feelings:** Starts with a strong apology and acknowledges the customer's frustration.
*   **Addresses Everything:** Doesn't ignore any of the customer's points.
*   **Reasonable Requests (and what we fulfill):**
    *   **Full Refund for Current Month:**  Absolutely reasonable.  We fulfill this immediately.
    *   **File Recovery:** Reasonable, *but* with a caveat.  We offer to *try* to recover the files, setting realistic expectations.
*   **Unreasonable Requests (and how we deflect):**
    *   **Free Premium for 12 Months:**  Unreasonable. We offer 3 months as a compromise.
    *   **CEO Personal Guarantee:**  Unreasonable and impossible. We deflect by escalating to engineering leadership.
    *   **Firing the Developer:**  Unreasonable and inappropriate. We deflect by focusing on the solution (fixing the code) rather than personnel.
*   **De-escalation:** Offers priority support, mentions monitoring Twitter (shows we're paying attention), and suggests a direct resolution to avoid further public discussion.
*   **Legal Mention:** Acknowledges the legal consideration without fueling the fire.  Focuses on a positive resolution.
* **Tone:** Empathetic, professional, and solution-oriented.



**Important Considerations:**

*   **Data Recovery:** Be *very* careful with promises about file recovery.  Under-promise and over-deliver.
*   **Engineering Escalation:** Actually escalate the issue to engineering!  The customer will likely follow up.
*   **Priority Support:** Make sure the priority support is *actually* prioritized.
*   **Documentation:** Document everything in the customer's account.
