### Spanish Translation

"Nuestro nuevo hosting Cloud9 te pondrá en las nubes! Como un buen amigo, ServerFarm está ahí para ti. No pongas todos los huevos en la misma cesta — nuestro sistema de failover multi-región significa que tus datos siempre estarán seguros. Con velocidades que harían que Usain Bolt se pusiera celoso, tus páginas cargarán antes de que puedas decir 'supercalifragilisticexpialidocious'."

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Cloud9/cloud nine"]**  I preserved the "Cloud9" pun as is. While the English idiom isn't directly present in Spanish, the concept of being "en las nubes" (in the clouds) to express extreme happiness is very common and understood.  Using "Cloud9" directly evokes the English meaning and is likely to be recognized as a playful reference by a tech-savvy audience.  A direct translation of "cloud nine" would sound awkward and lose the brand connection.

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Good Neighbor/State Farm"]** The "good neighbor" reference is very specific to US culture and State Farm.  Direct translation wouldn’t work. I adapted it to "Como un buen amigo" (Like a good friend). This conveys the same sense of reliability and support without relying on a US-specific brand association.  It's a more universally understood concept.

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Don't put all your eggs in one basket"]** This idiom translates very directly and naturally into Spanish as "No pongas todos los huevos en la misma cesta." It's a common proverb with the same meaning.

### Japanese Translation

"当社の新しいCloud9ホスティングは、あなたのウェブサイトを最高の気分にさせます！良い隣人として、ServerFarmがいつもそばにいます。卵を一つの籠に盛るな – 弊社のマルチリージョンフェイルオーバーにより、あなたのデータは常に安全です。ウサイン・ボルトも羨むほどのスピードで、あなたのページは「スーパーキャリフラジリスティックエクスピアリドーシャス」と言う間もなく読み込まれます。"

**(Translation in Romaji):** "Tosha no atarashii Cloud9 hosutingu wa, anata no webusaito o saikou no kibun ni sasemasu! Yoi rinjin to shite, ServerFarm ga itsumo soba ni imasu. Tamago o hitotsu no kago ni moru na – hei no maruchi riijon feiroubaa ni yori, anata no deeta wa tsuneni anzen desu. Usain Boruto mo urayamu hodo no supiido de, anata no peeji wa "suupaa kyari furajirisutikku ekusupiariidooshasu" to iu ma ni moku readaremasu."

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Cloud9/cloud nine"]** I preserved "Cloud9" directly.  While there isn't a perfect Japanese equivalent idiom, the concept of being in a very happy state is understood. Using the English term, especially within a tech context, can be seen as modern and appealing.  I added "最高の気分にさせます" (saikou no kibun ni sasemasu - makes your website feel the best) to reinforce the idea of happiness.

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Good Neighbor/State Farm"]** This was the most challenging element. The State Farm reference is completely lost on a Japanese audience. I translated it as "良い隣人として" (Yoi rinjin to shite - as a good neighbor).  This conveys the idea of being a reliable and helpful presence, which is the core message.  Japanese culture places a high value on community and helpfulness, so this resonates well.  I avoided trying to force a direct equivalent, as it would be meaningless.

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Don't put all your eggs in one basket"]** This idiom has a direct equivalent in Japanese: "卵を一つの籠に盛るな" (Tamago o hitotsu no kago ni moru na). It carries the same meaning of diversifying risk.  Using this established proverb ensures the message is clear and culturally appropriate.

**[TRANSLATOR NOTE: "Supercalifragilisticexpialidocious"]** I kept the word as is. It's a well-known, albeit unusual, English word.  Using it in the Japanese copy adds a touch of whimsy and reinforces the idea of incredibly fast speeds.  Trying to translate it would be impractical and lose the impact of the original.



**Important Considerations:**

*   **Target Audience:** These translations assume a tech-savvy audience who might be familiar with some English terminology.
*   **Marketing Tone:** I aimed for a friendly and approachable tone in both languages, consistent with the original copy.
*   **Localization vs. Translation:**  I leaned towards localization where necessary (e.g., the "good neighbor" adaptation) to ensure the message resonates with the target culture.
