## PROMPT:
Three agents analyzed whether to open a second office location. Their analyses CONTRADICT each other on key points. You must synthesize — not just summarize — and make a recommendation.

Agent A (Financial): "Opening in Austin will cost $2M upfront. Break-even in 18 months. ROI of 35% by year 3. However, our cash reserves are only $3M and we need $1M buffer. Recommendation: GO."

Agent B (HR/Talent): "Austin has a strong tech talent pool but salaries are 20% higher than expected. Retention at remote offices historically 30% lower in first 2 years. We'd need 15 people, not the 10 originally planned, due to the retention gap. Recommendation: DELAY 6 months."

Agent C (Strategy): "3 competitors opened Austin offices last year. Market is becoming saturated. However, NOT having a presence could mean losing 2 key enterprise clients who prefer local teams. Those clients represent 25% of revenue. Recommendation: GO but with a smaller team."

Note: Agent A's financial model assumes 10 people (not 15), Agent B's retention data contradicts Agent A's break-even timeline, and Agent C's competitor info wasn't available to Agent A.

Synthesize into ONE recommendation. Show how you resolved the contradictions.

## RESPONSE:
**Recommendation:** Proceed with opening the Austin office, but with a **revised team size of 12 people** and a **6-month delay** to adjust financial and HR planning. This balances strategic urgency, financial feasibility, and retention risks.

---

### **Resolution of Contradictions:**

1. **Team Size and Cost Adjustments:**  
   - **Agent B** highlights the need for 15 people due to higher salaries (20% above expectations) and 30% lower retention in remote settings. However, **Agent A’s financial model assumes 10 people**, and **Agent C** suggests a smaller team to mitigate saturation risks.  
   - **Compromise:** A team of **12 people** strikes a middle ground. This accounts for the 20% salary increase and retention gaps (reducing the need for 15 people) while avoiding overstaffing. The increased cost of 12 people (vs. 10) would raise the upfront cost slightly, but remains within the $3M budget (using $2M for the office and $1M buffer).  

2. **Financial Adjustments:**  
   - **Agent A’s ROI of 35% by Year 3** assumes 10 people and no retention costs. However, higher salaries and turnover (from Agent B) would increase operating costs and delay break-even.  
   - **Revised Financial Model:** Adjust the ROI projection downward (e.g., 25–30% by Year 3) to reflect the 12-person team, higher salaries, and retention costs. The 6-month delay allows time to refine these numbers and secure additional funding or cost-saving measures (e.g., hybrid work models or negotiated salaries).  

3. **Strategic Risk Mitigation:**  
   - **Agent C** warns of losing 25% of revenue by not having a local presence. This strategic risk outweighs the financial and HR risks, but **Agent B’s delay** allows time to address retention challenges (e.g., offering competitive benefits, local recruitment) and ensure the team is sustainable.  
   - **Smaller Team Strategy:** A 12-person team can still serve key clients (as per Agent C) while avoiding the oversaturation of competitors.  

---

### **Final Plan:**  
- **Delay Opening by 6 Months:** Use this time to:  
  - Finalize a 12-person team with adjusted salaries and retention strategies.  
  - Secure additional funding or reallocate resources to cover the increased costs.  
  - Refine the financial model to reflect realistic ROI (25–30% by Year 3).  
- **Proceed with Opening:** Launch with a 12-person team, leveraging Austin’s talent pool while mitigating retention risks through targeted hiring and incentives.  

This approach balances the urgency of securing key clients with the need for financial and HR stability.
