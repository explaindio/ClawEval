### **Party Problem: Minimum Number of People Who Drink All Three Beverages**

**Given:**
- 90 people drink **coffee**
- 80 people drink **tea**
- 70 people drink **juice**
- Total people: **100**

**Goal:**  
Find the **minimum number of people who drink all three beverages**.

---

#### **Solution Using Inclusion-Exclusion Principle**

Let:
- $ C $ = set of people who drink **coffee**
- $ T $ = set of people who drink **tea**
- $ J $ = set of people who drink **juice**

We are to find the **minimum** value of $ |C \cap T \cap J| $.

Using the **inclusion-exclusion principle**:

$$
|C \cup T \cup J| = |C| + |T| + |J| - |C \cap T| - |C \cap J| - |T \cap J| + |C \cap T \cap J|
$$

Assuming **everyone drinks at least one beverage**, $ |C \cup T \cup J| = 100 $, so:

$$
100 = 90 + 80 + 70 - (|C \cap T| + |C \cap J| + |T \cap J|) + |C \cap T \cap J|
$$

$$
100 = 240 - (|C \cap T| + |C \cap J| + |T \cap J|) + |C \cap T \cap J|
$$

$$
|C \cap T| + |C \cap J| + |T \cap J| - |C \cap T \cap J| = 140
$$

Let $ x = |C \cap T \cap J| $. Then:

$$
|C \cap T| + |C \cap J| + |T \cap J| = 140 + x
$$

To **minimize** $ x $, we must **maximize** the sum $ |C \cap T| + |C \cap J| + |T \cap J| $, but this is constrained by the sizes of the sets.

Each person can be in **at most two sets** to avoid overcounting. The maximum number of "slots" is $ 2 \times 100 = 200 $, but the total number of slots is $ 90 + 80 + 70 = 240 $, which is **40 more than 200**.

Each person in **all three sets** adds **one extra slot** (since they are counted in all three sets). Therefore, at least **40 people must be in all three sets** to account for the excess.
